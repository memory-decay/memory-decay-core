New mechanism: activation-adaptive sigmoid steepness.

The sigmoid floor's steepness (k) now scales with current activation level:
- High activation (1.0): effective_k = 20 (standard, no change)
- Low activation (0.5): effective_k = 30 (50% steeper)
- Very low (0.3): effective_k = 36 (80% steeper)

This creates a "clutch" effect: memories approaching their floor get steeper
floor response, making it HARDER to slip below the floor. Memories well above
their floor decay at standard rate.

This is fundamentally different from all prior approaches:
- Prior "activation-boosted floor" (exp_0346 attempt 2): blended activation INTO floor value → destroyed recall
- This approach: makes floor RESPOND to activation level via steepness, not value

Hypothesis: The "clutch" should prevent the recall plateaus observed in all
prior experiments. Instead of decaying to ~0.40 and staying there, the
near-floor memories should get extra protection, pushing recall higher.

Contrast with exp_0338's assoc_boost=2.0 which boosted recall to 0.4146 but
destroyed correlation. The adaptive steepness approach should boost recall
WITHOUT correlation cost because it's modifying the floor physics, not
manipulating retrieval rankings.
