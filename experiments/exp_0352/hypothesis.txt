Combine stability_decay=0.003 (from exp_0351) with lambda_fact=0.0095 (higher base decay).

Exp_0351 showed that stability_decay=0.003 gives positive precision_lift (0.0024) but
reduced recall (0.382 vs 0.402). The hypothesis: the slower stability decay kept too
many weak memories alive, crowding out correct ones in top-k.

This experiment compensates by raising lambda_fact from 0.008 to 0.0095 (+19%), which
should restore recall to ~0.40 while preserving the precision_lift benefit from
slower stability decay. The net effect should be positive.

Key insight: precision_lift > 0 is achievable (first time in many experiments).
The challenge is getting it without sacrificing recall.
