Slow down stability decay: stability_decay 0.005 → 0.003 (40% reduction).

With lower stability_decay, memories that receive reinforcement maintain their stability
longer, creating stronger long-term retention. This means:
- Reinforced memories get a lasting boost to their decay resistance
- The "dip and recover" pattern (tick 100→140) may shift upward
- Late-tick recall (tick 200) may improve

All other parameters stay identical to best (exp_0315) to isolate this effect.

The stability_decay=0.005 baseline was never systematically varied in prior experiments
(focused on decay_fn changes). This tests whether the reinforcement-stability
loop is under-optimized.

Rejected by 17+ experiments: floor/imporance/retention/decay form changes all
converged to exp_0315 as local optimum. This is the first attempt to tune
simulation-level reinforcement parameters.
