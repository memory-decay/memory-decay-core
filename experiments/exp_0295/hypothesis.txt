Hypothesis: The previous attempt pruned too aggressively, dropping all activations below retrievable thresholds and destroying recall. This experiment introduces a linear floor spanning from 0.05 (for zero impact) to 0.45 (for max impact). By setting `floor_min` to 0.05, low-impact memories will naturally decay below the 0.2 evaluation threshold, enabling pruning. Meanwhile, a `floor_max` of 0.45 ensures high-impact memories remain retrievable across most thresholds. The base decay rate is softened to prevent instant collapse.
