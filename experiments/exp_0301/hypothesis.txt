Jost's Law decay with impact-based sigmoid floor.

The baseline exponential decay uses a constant rate regardless of current
activation level. Jost's Law states that older (lower-activation) memories
decay more slowly — mathematically, the decay rate is proportional to
activation itself: da/dt = -lambda * a^2, yielding hyperbolic-like behavior.

Combined with a sigmoid floor that protects high-impact memories, this should:
1. Produce "steep early, gradual late" curves (improved plausibility/correlation)
2. Keep important memories above threshold longer (improved recall)
3. Let low-impact memories die faster (improved precision_lift via pruning)

The activation-weighted ranking (new in graph.py) means that even partially
decayed memories contribute less to ranking, so selective preservation of
high-impact nodes should directly improve MRR.
