Zero-impact memories must decay below 0.30 at tick 200 to pass strict validation.
The current Jost's Law decay still keeps impact=0 items at ~0.42 because the sigmoid floor
for zero importance is small but combined with the high activation start (0.5), the excess decay
per tick is too slow (effective_lambda / retention = 0.009/1.0 = 0.009).

Fix: bypass the sigmoid floor entirely for impact=0, use pure exponential with
effective_lambda * 3.0 ≈ 0.027 for facts. This gives 0.5 * exp(-5.4) ≈ 0.0045 by tick 200,
well below the 0.30 threshold.

This preserves the Jost+sigmoid floor behavior for all items with impact > 0.
