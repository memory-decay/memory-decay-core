Replace power-law Jost decay (excess^jost_power) with tanh-based decay (tanh(excess/scale)).

Power-law decay (jost_power=4) has very asymmetric behavior: negligible decay near floor,
but extremely aggressive decay when far from floor. Tanh provides intermediate behavior:
bounded decay rate (never exceeds lambda) while still being proportional to excess.
This is more biologically plausible and may avoid the "cliff" behavior of power-law.

Parameters: lambda_fact=0.005 (reduced from 0.008 to compensate for tanh's higher
near-floor decay rate vs power^jost), tanh_scale=0.15 (transition point where
tanh starts saturating).

Rejected: log decay (recall collapsed), sqrt retention (hurt recall), sigmoid_k/mid variations,
activation-boosted floor, all failed to beat best (exp_0315) after 12 consecutive attempts.
