Separated impact/stability roles in the decay formula.

Currently (exp_0315): importance = (impact*α + stability*ρ)/(α+ρ) controls BOTH
decay rate (via retention) AND floor position (via sigmoid). This means high-stability
memories with low impact get low importance = low floor = vulnerable to erasure.

Separated design:
- Impact (alone) drives retention = decay rate
- Stability (alone) drives sigmoid floor = protection level
- High-stability/low-impact memories: slow decay + high floor = can accumulate
  activation through reinforcement without being penalized by low impact

This is mathematically different from reweighting α and ρ — the coupling is
fundamentally broken. A low-impact memory that gets reinforced 10 times can now
maintain high activation even though its impact is low.

If this doesn't improve things, the coupling in the original formula was actually
beneficial and there's no path within the current architecture.
