Add stability to the floor calculation: floor = base_floor * (1 + stability_floor_scale * stability).
Memories that have been reinforced (higher stability) get a higher floor, protecting
them from excessive decay. Train memories (directly reactivated) have high stability
and keep high activation. Test memories with associative reinforcement get moderate
stability and moderate floor. This creates a stability-based activation ordering that
could correlate with recall (more connected memories → more associations → more stability).
