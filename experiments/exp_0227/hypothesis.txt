Activation-dependent decay rate: memories at high activation decay faster, creating
a convergent behavior toward a mid-range target. This is different from floor-based
decay (which decays excess above floor). Here, the rate itself scales with activation:
effective_rate = lam * activation / combined. This creates softer, more gradual
convergence and potentially smoother forgetting curves. Higher-impact memories still
decay slower (through combined factor) but the rate scaling prevents extremely slow
decay at low activations.
