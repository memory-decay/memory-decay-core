Replace linear importance (weighted average) with power-mean importance.

The current linear importance = (imp*α + stab*ρ)/(α+ρ) is dominated by the higher
value. Power-mean with p=0.5 emphasizes the LOWER of (impact, stability) — a
memory must have BOTH high impact AND high stability to get a high floor.

This creates stronger selectivity: (impact=0.9, stability=0.1) gets importance 0.40
(vs 0.62 with linear), meaning "flash-in-the-pan" high-impact memories get lower
floor protection. (impact=0.5, stability=0.5) gets 0.50 (same as arithmetic mean).

Hypothesis: this bottleneck principle should improve correlation by making the floor
better track "genuinely important" memories, potentially improving both retrieval
and plausibility scores.
